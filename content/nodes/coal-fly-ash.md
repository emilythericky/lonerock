## Depth

**TVA Kingston Coal Ash Spill, December 22, 2008**
1.1 billion gallons of coal fly ash slurry. 300 acres inundated. Over 5 million cubic yards of material removed during cleanup, 2009-2014. Cleanup workers — many from outside the region — have since reported elevated rates of cancer, respiratory disease, and neurological symptoms. TVA's contractor, Jacobs Engineering, has faced wrongful death lawsuits. As of 2024, litigation is ongoing.

**Coal fly ash composition**
Contains arsenic, mercury, chromium, lead, selenium, boron, thallium, and other heavy metals in concentrations that vary by coal source. When dry, it is a fine particulate that can be inhaled. When wet, it behaves as a liquid. The EPA classified it as a special waste in 1993 but declined to regulate it as a hazardous waste under RCRA — a decision reversed in 2015, partially reversed again in 2020, reinstated in 2024.

**Parsley as abortifacient**
*Petroselinum crispum*. Used historically across many cultures. Apiol, the active compound in parsley seed oil, causes uterine contractions. Efficacy as an abortifacient is inconsistent and the doses required for effect are close to toxic doses. This is the information available in the absence of medical care. This is what women have always known.
