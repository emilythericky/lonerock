## Depth

**Coal ash contamination, Grundy Lakes**
Coal combustion residuals (CCRs) contain heavy metals including arsenic, selenium, chromium, cadmium, mercury, and lead. Unlike organic pollutants, heavy metals do not biodegrade. They cycle through aquatic food chains indefinitely.

**Box turtle (*Terrapene carolina*)**
Tennessee state reptile. Population in significant decline due to habitat fragmentation and road mortality. Long-lived (up to 100 years), slow-reproducing. Each individual lost to a dirt bike represents years or decades of accumulated life.

**"How small do we have to be to be so often?"**
The question recurs across the manuscript. It is not answered. It is asked again in Deep Creek, in the Coal Fly Ash Slurry Spill poem. The repetition is structural — a recurrence like water recurring, like impurities cycling through limestone that was supposed to filter them.

**The Grundy County watershed**
Grundy County sits at the headwaters of the Sequatchie River and multiple tributaries. What enters the water here travels. The stockade is not a closed system. The ponds are not closed systems. The contamination moves downstream into communities that may not know the source.
