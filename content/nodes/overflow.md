## Surface

**Overflow**

A guilt at brim with yet

the unifying water 

table this single shore 

					of earth and of speech Son

of cupping his

hands beneath 

what's left of it

## Depth

**Grundy County groundwater**
The fractured karst aquifer system of the Cumberland Plateau connects surface water and groundwater in ways that make contamination tracking difficult. Coal ash heavy metals have been detected in private wells in Grundy County. The state has not conducted comprehensive groundwater testing of the Grundy Lakes area.

**Cupped hands**
This is the oldest vessel. Before ceramic, before gourd, before the bark cup — the cupped hands, the body's own capacity to hold water. What the poem holds to is this: even in the contaminated watershed, even at the site of the unacknowledged crime, the body still needs water and still cups its hands for it. The gesture is not ironic. It is what we have.
