## Depth

**Legal personhood and convict status**
Under convict leasing, the incarcerated lost civil standing. They could not sue, could not contract, could not own property. The Thirteenth Amendment's exception clause converted persons into instruments. This was not metaphorical. It had legal force.

**Water moccasin misidentification**
*Nerodia sipedon* (northern water snake) is the most commonly killed "cottonmouth" in Tennessee. It is not venomous. It is dark, swims, and basks near water — the same behaviors as *Agkistrodon piscivorus*. Thousands are killed annually on the basis of species confusion. The mistake is systematic, not individual.

**Names in the archive**
Camille Westmont's archaeological work at Lone Rock recovered partial records. Some names are in the archive. Most are not. The archive reflects what was considered worth recording. What was not considered worth recording includes: most of the men held here, most of what they said, most of what they knew, most of what they were before they were entered into the record as convict numbers.
