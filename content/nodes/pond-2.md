## Surface

**Pond 2**

All the things we don't believe in 

anymore there in the air around us 

as if a made

bat trolling its sonar beside the brittle murmurs 

of an unfolding bone 

"a quorum of cicadas" 

the generations switching sides again 

their spectrum scraped through 

a width of leafed-out 

black walnut nutted spasm 

maybe it does work 

maybe it does work how JayVe says it does: 

just listen in 

and spread out the absent 

evenly there 

"where its been done"

## Depth

**JayVe Montgomery**
Tennessee jazz musician and breath artist. Collaborator on the *Mrs. Grundy Lone Rock* performance project. Has performed with Ed Roberson, Claudia Rankine, André 3000. His understanding of Black electronica as sonic embodiment of what ancestors might have heard in the hold of a slave ship — the reverberation of the ocean, tidal motion, whale song — is central to the project's acoustic imagination.

**Periodical cicadas (genus *Magicicada*)**
Emerge on 13- or 17-year cycles. Prime numbers. The mathematical relationship between the cycles is still incompletely understood. The Cumberland Plateau hosts populations of both Brood XIX (13-year) and Brood XIII (17-year). A simultaneous emergence of both occurs approximately once every 221 years.

**Sycamore mycorrhizal networks**
*Platanus occidentalis* forms extensive mycorrhizal associations with fungi in the genus *Glomus* and related species. The network connects root systems, mediates nutrient transfer, and — over long timescales — incorporates the matter of whatever decomposes within it. The sycamores at the stockade burial site are between 80 and 130 years old.
