## Depth

**Tennessee Coal, Iron and Railroad Company (TCIR)**
Incorporated 1881. Acquired Sewanee Coal and Coke Company properties 1886. Built the Lone Rock Stockade outside Tracy City, Grundy County, Tennessee, 1883. Later absorbed by U.S. Steel, 1907.

**Convict leasing in Tennessee**
Inaugurated 1871, six years after the end of the Civil War. The state leased convicts to private companies for labor. Companies paid a per-diem fee; the state was relieved of the cost of incarceration. The leased workers received nothing. Their labor built the New South economy.

**The Thirteenth Amendment exception**
"Neither slavery nor involuntary servitude, *except as a punishment for crime whereof the party shall have been duly convicted*, shall exist within the United States." Ratified 1865. The exception was not an oversight.

**Camille Westmont, *An Archaeology of Convict Leasing in the American South***
"Over the 25 years prisoners were sent to Tracy City, over 75% were categorized as Black." The stockade held "at least 3,500 individuals" between 1884 and 1896.

**The Grundy Lakes Day Use Area**
Current name of the site. South Cumberland State Park. The ponds are tailings ponds. They are now used for swimming, fishing, picnicking. The coke oven arches still stand along the south edge of the water, colonized by ferns.
