## Depth

**Karst hydrology, Cumberland Plateau**
The plateau is capped by sandstone over soluble limestone and dolomite. Surface water sinks through joints and sinkholes into subterranean drainage. Contaminants introduced at the surface — coal ash, fuel, mine runoff — travel through the karst aquifer in ways that are difficult to predict or trace. Dye-trace studies repeatedly show water emerging miles from where it entered.

**Eurycea lucifuga — cave salamander**
A lungless salamander (family Plethodontidae) of the eastern US karst. Orange with dark spots; long-tailed; associated with cave entrances and limestone springs. Not blind, but light-avoiding. The epithet *lucifuga* = "flees the light."

**Tennessee Coal, Iron and Railroad (TCI / "TN coal and Fe")**
Iron (Fe) and coal were the twin extractions. Coke ovens drove volatiles from coal; the resulting coke fed iron furnaces. Silica dust from associated quarrying and the silica content of coal seams caused silicosis among miners. The convict-leased labor force absorbed the most dangerous work.

**"Son of Ham"**
The "Curse of Ham" (Genesis 9:20–27) was used for centuries as a biblical justification for the enslavement of African peoples. Its invocation here folds the theological alibi of slavery into the industrial machinery that consumed Black labor in the mines.

**doghole mine**
Industry term for a small, shallow, often informal coal mine — typically a low drift entry a worker could barely stand in, worked with minimal capital and minimal safety. Common across Appalachia.
